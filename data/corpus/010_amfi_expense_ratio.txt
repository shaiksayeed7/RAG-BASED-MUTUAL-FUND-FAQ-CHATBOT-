AMFI - EXPENSE RATIO IN MUTUAL FUNDS

What is Expense Ratio?
The Expense Ratio (also called Total Expense Ratio or TER) is the annual fee charged by a mutual fund scheme for managing your money. It is expressed as a percentage of the fund's Average Weekly Net Assets (AWNA). The expense ratio covers fund management fees, administrative expenses, marketing costs, registrar fees, and other operational costs.

How is Expense Ratio calculated?
Expense Ratio = Total Annual Expenses ÷ Average Net Assets of the Scheme × 100

For example, if a fund has average net assets of Rs. 1,000 crore and annual expenses of Rs. 15 crore, the expense ratio is 1.5%.

How does Expense Ratio affect my returns?
The expense ratio is deducted daily from the NAV. If a fund earns 12% gross return and has an expense ratio of 1.5%, the net return to investors is approximately 10.5%. Lower expense ratio generally means better net returns to investors, all else being equal.

SEBI Limits on Expense Ratio:
As per SEBI circular dated September 18, 2018, the maximum permissible TER (Total Expense Ratio) is:
- Equity Schemes with AUM up to Rs. 500 crore: 2.25%
- Equity Schemes with AUM Rs. 500-750 crore: 2.00%
- Equity Schemes with AUM Rs. 750-2000 crore: 1.75%
- Equity Schemes with AUM Rs. 2000-5000 crore: 1.60%
- Equity Schemes with AUM above Rs. 5000 crore: 1.50%
- Additional 0.30% allowed for new inflows from cities beyond top 30 (B30 cities)
- Direct Plans must have a lower TER than Regular Plans by at least the trail commission paid

Regular Plan vs Direct Plan Expense Ratio:
- Regular Plan: Includes distributor commission (trail fee), so TER is higher
- Direct Plan: No distributor commission, so TER is lower
- The difference between Regular and Direct plan TER is the distributor commission
