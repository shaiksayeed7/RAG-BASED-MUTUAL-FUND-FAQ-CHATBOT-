HDFC MUTUAL FUND - TAX DOCUMENTS

HDFC Mutual Fund provides the following tax-related documents for investors:

1. Capital Gains Statement
Purpose: Used for filing Income Tax Return (ITR) to declare gains from mutual fund redemptions.
How to download:
- Login to https://www.hdfcfund.com → Tax Reports → Capital Gains Statement
- Or visit: https://www.camsonline.com → Select Capital Gains Statement
- Available formats: PDF, Excel (XLS)
Covers: All redemptions, switches, and IDCW (dividend) payouts during the financial year

2. 80C Tax Saving Statement (ELSS)
Purpose: Proof of ELSS investment for Section 80C deduction claim.
Applicable Scheme: HDFC ELSS Tax Saver
How to download:
- Login to HDFC Fund → Tax Reports → 80C Statement
- Or use CAMS: https://www.camsonline.com → Investor Services → Tax Statements
- Shows: Investment amount, date, units allotted for the financial year

3. TDS Certificate (Form 16A)
Purpose: For NRI investors (Non-Resident Indians) who have TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) on redemptions.
For Resident Indians: TDS is generally not applicable on mutual fund redemptions (as of FY 2024-25).
How to access: Login to HDFC Fund → Tax Reports → TDS Certificate

Tax Rates Summary for Mutual Fund Gains (FY 2024-25):
Equity Mutual Funds (including HDFC Top 100, Flexi Cap, ELSS, Mid-Cap):
- STCG (Short-Term Capital Gains): 20% (if held < 1 year; 3 years for ELSS)
- LTCG (Long-Term Capital Gains): 12.5% on gains above Rs. 1,25,000 per year (if held > 1 year; > 3 years for ELSS)

Debt Mutual Funds (if held > 3 years purchased before April 1, 2023):
- LTCG: 20% with indexation
Note: From April 1, 2023, gains from debt mutual funds are taxed at slab rates irrespective of holding period.
