SEBI - TOTAL EXPENSE RATIO (TER) OF MUTUAL FUND SCHEMES

SEBI Circular: SEBI/HO/IMD/DF3/CIR/P/2018/197
Date: October 5, 2018
Subject: Total Expense Ratio (TER) of Mutual Fund Schemes

This circular revised the TER (Total Expense Ratio) limits for mutual fund schemes in India.

Maximum Permissible TER for Equity Schemes:
- AUM up to Rs. 500 crore: 2.25%
- AUM Rs. 500 crore to Rs. 750 crore: 2.00%
- AUM Rs. 750 crore to Rs. 2,000 crore: 1.75%
- AUM Rs. 2,000 crore to Rs. 5,000 crore: 1.60%
- AUM Rs. 5,000 crore to Rs. 10,000 crore: 1.50%
- AUM Rs. 10,000 crore to Rs. 50,000 crore: TER of the scheme calculated as per formula
- AUM above Rs. 50,000 crore: 1.05% (with some additional allowances)

Note: For large equity funds (AUM > Rs. 50,000 crore), the TER reduces as AUM grows. The formula ensures larger funds pass savings to investors.

Additional TER Allowances:
1. B30 Cities Incentive: Additional 0.30% TER allowed for inflows from beyond top 30 cities (B30 cities) to encourage financial inclusion
2. Performance-based incentive for liquid/overnight/money market funds: Not applicable to equity funds

Direct Plan TER:
- Direct Plan TER = Regular Plan TER minus the trail commission/distribution expense
- Direct Plans cannot have higher TER than Regular Plans
- AMCs must pass through the savings from no distribution expense to Direct Plan investors

Transparency Requirements:
1. AMCs must disclose TER of each scheme on their website daily
2. AMFI maintains a central repository of TER: https://www.amfiindia.com/expense-ratio
3. Any change in TER must be notified to investors via email/SMS at least 3 working days before the change

SEBI Prohibition on Brokerage in TER:
As per SEBI circular, brokerage and transaction costs above 0.12% per annum cannot be charged to TER for equity schemes.

Practical Implication for HDFC Funds:
HDFC Flexi Cap Fund (AUM ~Rs. 65,847 crore) and HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund (AUM ~Rs. 75,212 crore) qualify as large funds. Their actual TER (1.46% and 1.68% regular plans) are within the SEBI limits adjusted for their AUM sizes.
