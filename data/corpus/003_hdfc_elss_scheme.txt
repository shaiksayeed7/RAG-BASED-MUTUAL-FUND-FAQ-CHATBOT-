Investment Objective:
To generate long-term capital appreciation from a portfolio that is invested predominantly in equity and equity-related instruments.

Asset Allocation:
- Equity and Equity Related Instruments: 80%–100%
- Debt and Money Market Instruments: 0%–20%

Statutory Lock-in Period: 3 years from the date of allotment
(Mandatory for all investments made under ELSS. Units cannot be redeemed, switched-out, or pledged before 3 years.)

Tax Benefits:
- Investment up to Rs. 1,50,000 per financial year qualifies for deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961.
- Tax benefit is available only under the old tax regime.
- After 3-year lock-in, LTCG tax of 12.5% applies on gains exceeding Rs. 1,25,000 per year.

Minimum Investment:
- Minimum Lump Sum: Rs. 500
- Minimum SIP (Systematic Investment Plan): Rs. 500 per installment
- Minimum SIP Installments: 3

Exit Load: Nil
(No exit load since all units are subject to a mandatory 3-year lock-in period.)

- Regular Plan: 1.73% per annum
- Direct Plan: 1.02% per annum

Riskometer: Very High Risk
(This means the fund is suitable for investors with very high risk tolerance. Principal is at very high risk.)

Plans Available: Regular Plan, Direct Plan
Options: Growth, IDCW (Income Distribution cum Capital Withdrawal - applicable after 3-year lock-in)

Important ELSS Lock-in Rules:
1. Each SIP installment is subject to its own 3-year lock-in from the date of allotment of that installment.
2. Units allotted in January 2022 can be redeemed from January 2025.
3. Units allotted in February 2022 can be redeemed from February 2025, and so on.
4. Partial redemption is allowed after lock-in, subject to units being unlocked.
