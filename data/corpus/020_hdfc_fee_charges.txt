HDFC MUTUAL FUND - FEE AND CHARGES SCHEDULE

Summary of charges applicable to HDFC Mutual Fund schemes:

ENTRY LOAD: NIL
(Entry load was abolished by SEBI for all mutual fund schemes from August 1, 2009.)

EXIT LOAD (Scheme-wise):

HDFC Top 100 Fund:
- Within 1 year (365 days): 1.00%
- After 1 year: Nil
- Exit load is not applicable on: Units allotted through dividend reinvestment

HDFC Flexi Cap Fund:
- Within 1 year (365 days): 1.00%
- After 1 year: Nil

HDFC ELSS Tax Saver:
- Exit Load: NIL (all units are locked in for 3 years; no exit load after lock-in ends)

HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund:
- Within 1 year (365 days): 1.00%
- After 1 year: Nil

SWITCH LOAD:
- Same as Exit Load applies when switching between schemes
- Switching from equity scheme within 1 year: 1% exit load on the amount switched out
- No load on switching between growth and IDCW options within the same scheme

STAMP DUTY (Effective July 1, 2020):
- On Purchase of Mutual Fund units: 0.005% of investment amount
- On Switch (out): 0.015% of switch amount
- Governed by Stamp Duty Act
- Collected at the time of unit allotment

TRANSACTION CHARGES (Distributor-based):
- For investments through distributors (Groww, banks, etc.)
- For investments ≥ Rs. 10,000 via distributor: Rs. 150 one-time for new investor (only in Regular Plan)
- For existing investors: Rs. 100 (only in Regular Plan, first investment ≥ Rs. 10,000)
- NOT applicable for Direct Plan investments
- NOT applicable for SIP investments where total commitment < Rs. 10,000

DEMAT TRANSACTION CHARGES:
- If you hold mutual fund units in demat form through NSDL/CDSL, annual demat charges apply as per your broker's fee structure
- This is separate from the fund's own charges

SERVICE TAX / GST on Expense Ratio:
- GST (18%) is applicable on the investment management fee component of the TER
- The expense ratio already includes GST; it's included in the published TER

For the most current fee schedule, visit: https://www.hdfcfund.com/loads
