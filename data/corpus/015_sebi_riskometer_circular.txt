SEBI CIRCULAR ON RISKOMETER FOR MUTUAL FUNDS

SEBI Circular Number: SEBI/HO/IMD/IMD-II DOF3/P/CIR/2020/198
Date: October 5, 2020
Subject: Product Labeling in Mutual Funds – Risk-O-Meter

Background:
SEBI previously had a 5-level riskometer. This circular updated it to a 6-level riskometer to provide more granular risk information to investors.

Updated Risk Levels (Effective January 1, 2021):
1. Low - Principal at Low Risk
2. Low to Moderate - Principal at Low to Moderate Risk
3. Moderate - Principal at Moderate Risk
4. Moderately High - Principal at Moderately High Risk
5. High - Principal at High Risk
6. Very High - Principal at Very High Risk

Key Provisions of the Circular:
1. Monthly Evaluation: AMCs must evaluate and disclose the riskometer for each scheme at the end of every month.
2. Change Notification: If the riskometer level changes, AMCs must communicate to all unit holders via email or SMS within 10 working days of the end of the month in which the change occurred.
3. Website Disclosure: AMCs must display the riskometer on their website and in all scheme communications.
4. Factsheet Disclosure: Riskometer must be prominently displayed in all factsheets.

Methodology for Riskometer:
For Equity Schemes:
- Market Capitalization: Large cap = Lower risk; Mid/Small cap = Higher risk
- Impact Cost: Higher impact cost = Higher risk
- The methodology assigns scores that determine the final risk level

For HDFC Mutual Fund schemes assessed under this circular:
- HDFC Top 100 Fund: VERY HIGH (pure equity large cap)
- HDFC Flexi Cap Fund: VERY HIGH (pure equity across market caps)
- HDFC ELSS Tax Saver: VERY HIGH (pure equity ELSS)
- HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund: VERY HIGH (pure equity mid cap)

Disclaimer in SEBI circular:
"The risk-o-meter shall be evaluated and disclosed at the end of every month. In case there is a change in the risk-o-meter of the scheme, the same shall be communicated by way of Notice cum Addendum and by intimating the investors through email/SMS."
