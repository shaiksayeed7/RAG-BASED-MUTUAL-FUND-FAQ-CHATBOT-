AMFI - RISKOMETER FOR MUTUAL FUNDS

What is the Riskometer?
The Riskometer is a standardized risk measurement tool for mutual fund schemes in India, mandated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India). It visually depicts the risk level of a mutual fund scheme on a dial (like a speedometer) ranging from Low to Very High risk.

Riskometer Risk Levels (6 levels as per SEBI Circular 2020):
1. Low - Principal at low risk
2. Low to Moderate - Principal at low to moderate risk
3. Moderate - Principal at moderate risk
4. Moderately High - Principal at moderately high risk
5. High - Principal at high risk
6. Very High - Principal at very high risk

SEBI Circular on Riskometer (October 5, 2020):
SEBI circular SEBI/HO/IMD/IMD-II DOF3/P/CIR/2020/198 mandated updated riskometer norms effective January 1, 2021. Key changes:
- Riskometer must be evaluated and disclosed monthly
- If the riskometer level changes, investors must be informed via email/SMS
- Riskometer is based on objective parameters like credit risk, interest rate risk, liquidity risk for debt funds, and market cap allocation for equity funds

Riskometer for HDFC Mutual Fund Schemes:
- HDFC Top 100 Fund: VERY HIGH RISK
  (Large cap equity fund; equity investments carry very high market risk)
- HDFC Flexi Cap Fund: VERY HIGH RISK
  (Multi-cap equity fund; equity investments carry very high market risk)
- HDFC ELSS Tax Saver: VERY HIGH RISK
  (Equity ELSS fund; equity investments carry very high market risk)
- HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund: VERY HIGH RISK
  (Mid cap equity fund; equity investments carry very high market risk)

What does "Very High Risk" mean for equity funds?
A "Very High Risk" riskometer means that the investor's principal is at very high risk due to market volatility. The value of investment can go down significantly in a short period. This does NOT mean the fund is of poor quality—it simply reflects the inherent volatility of equity markets.

Are all equity mutual funds "Very High Risk"?
Most equity mutual funds are rated "Very High Risk" on the riskometer because:
- Equity markets are inherently volatile
- The value of equity holdings can fluctuate significantly
- Short-term losses are common even in well-managed equity funds

Where to see the Riskometer?
- Every scheme's factsheet, KIM, and SID must display the riskometer
- AMC website scheme pages
- AMFI scheme performance page: https://www.amfiindia.com/schemes-performance
- Groww fund detail pages show the riskometer for each fund
