SEBI - CATEGORIZATION AND RATIONALIZATION OF MUTUAL FUND SCHEMES

SEBI Circular: SEBI/HO/IMD/DF3/CIR/P/2017/114
Date: October 6, 2017 (Updated in 2018)
Subject: Categorization and Rationalization of Mutual Fund Schemes

This SEBI circular defined standard categories for mutual fund schemes to bring uniformity and avoid multiple schemes of similar nature from the same AMC.

Equity Fund Categories Relevant to HDFC Corpus:

1. Large Cap Fund:
- Definition: Open-ended equity scheme predominantly investing in large cap stocks
- Investment in Equity & Equity Related Instruments of Large Cap Companies: Minimum 80% of total assets
- Large Cap defined as: 1st to 100th company in terms of full market capitalization (AMFI list)
- HDFC Scheme: HDFC Top 100 Fund

2. Flexi Cap Fund (Added in 2020):
- Definition: Open-ended dynamic equity scheme investing across large cap, mid cap, small cap stocks
- Investment in Equity & Equity Related Instruments: Minimum 65% of total assets
- No restriction on market cap allocation (fund manager can allocate freely)
- HDFC Scheme: HDFC Flexi Cap Fund

3. Mid Cap Fund:
- Definition: Open-ended equity scheme predominantly investing in mid cap stocks
- Investment in Equity & Equity Related Instruments of Mid Cap Companies: Minimum 65% of total assets
- Mid Cap defined as: 101st to 250th company in terms of full market capitalization (AMFI list)
- HDFC Scheme: HDFC Mid-Cap Opportunities Fund

4. ELSS (Equity Linked Saving Scheme):
- Definition: Open-ended equity linked saving scheme with a statutory lock-in of 3 years and tax benefit
- Investment in Equity & Equity Related Instruments: Minimum 80% of total assets
- Statutory lock-in: 3 years from date of allotment
- Tax benefit: Eligible for Section 80C deduction up to Rs. 1,50,000
- HDFC Scheme: HDFC ELSS Tax Saver

Market Capitalization Definitions (AMFI List, updated bi-annually):
- Large Cap: 1st to 100th company by full market cap
- Mid Cap: 101st to 250th company by full market cap
- Small Cap: 251st company onwards by full market cap
- AMFI updates this list every six months: https://www.amfiindia.com/research-information/other-data/categorization-of-stocks

One Scheme Per Category Rule:
As per SEBI circular, each AMC can offer only ONE scheme per category (with some exceptions). This prevents fund houses from having multiple overlapping schemes.
