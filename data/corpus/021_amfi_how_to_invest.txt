AMFI - HOW TO INVEST IN MUTUAL FUNDS IN INDIA

Step-by-Step Guide to Invest in Mutual Funds:

Step 1: Complete KYC (Know Your Customer)
KYC is mandatory for all mutual fund investors in India.
Documents Required:
- PAN Card (mandatory)
- Proof of Address (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, Driver's License, Utility Bill)
- Photograph (passport size)
How to do KYC:
- Online via KYC Registration Agency (KRA): CVL KRA, CAMS KRA, Karvy KRA, NDML KRA
- Online e-KYC using Aadhaar OTP: Through AMC website or distributor
- KYC Portal: https://www.camskra.com or https://www.cvlkra.com

Step 2: Choose Investment Mode
- Direct Plan: Invest directly through AMC website (e.g., hdfcfund.com) - lower expense ratio
- Regular Plan: Invest through distributor/agent (e.g., Groww, bank) - higher expense ratio but with advisory support

Step 3: Select Scheme
Choose a scheme based on your investment goal, time horizon, and risk tolerance.
AMFI Scheme Selector: https://www.amfiindia.com/scheme-performance
Fund categories available: Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, Flexi Cap, ELSS, Debt, Hybrid, etc.

Step 4: Complete Investment
- Online: AMC website or distributor app (like Groww)
- Offline: Submit physical form at AMC branch or investor service center

Step 5: Provide Bank Mandate (for SIP)
For SIP, provide NACH/ECS mandate to auto-debit bank account.

Important Notes:
- No entry load is charged by any mutual fund in India (abolished August 2009 by SEBI)
- A PAN card is mandatory for all mutual fund investments
- All mutual funds are regulated by SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)
- AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India) is the industry body: https://www.amfiindia.com
