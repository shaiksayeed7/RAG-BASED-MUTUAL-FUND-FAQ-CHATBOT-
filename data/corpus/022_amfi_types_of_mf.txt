AMFI - TYPES OF MUTUAL FUNDS IN INDIA

Mutual funds in India are categorized by SEBI into the following broad types:

1. EQUITY MUTUAL FUNDS
These funds invest primarily in stocks (equity shares).
- Large Cap Funds: Invest ≥80% in top 100 companies by market cap
- Mid Cap Funds: Invest ≥65% in companies ranked 101-250 by market cap
- Small Cap Funds: Invest ≥65% in companies ranked 251 and beyond
- Flexi Cap Funds: Invest ≥65% in equity across all market caps (no cap restriction)
- Multi Cap Funds: Invest ≥25% each in large, mid, and small cap
- ELSS (Equity Linked Saving Scheme): ≥80% in equity; 3-year lock-in; eligible for 80C tax deduction
- Sectoral/Thematic Funds: Invest in specific sectors (banking, IT, healthcare, etc.)

2. DEBT MUTUAL FUNDS
These funds invest in fixed-income instruments like bonds, T-bills, commercial paper.
Types: Liquid Funds, Overnight Funds, Ultra Short Duration, Short Duration, Long Duration, etc.
Risk: Generally lower risk than equity, but subject to interest rate risk and credit risk.

3. HYBRID FUNDS
These funds invest in both equity and debt.
- Balanced Advantage Fund (BAF): Dynamically manages equity-debt allocation
- Conservative Hybrid: 10-25% in equity, 75-90% in debt
- Aggressive Hybrid: 65-80% in equity, 20-35% in debt

4. INDEX FUNDS AND ETFs
- Passive funds that track an index (NIFTY 50, NIFTY 100, etc.)
- Lower expense ratio than actively managed funds

5. FUND OF FUNDS
- Invest in other mutual fund schemes
- E.g., International Fund of Funds investing in overseas equity funds

Key Concepts:
- NAV (Net Asset Value): Price per unit of the mutual fund, calculated at end of each business day
- SIP (Systematic Investment Plan): Regular investment at fixed intervals
- Lump Sum: One-time investment
- Redemption: Withdrawal of invested money by selling units back to AMC

SEBI-Registered AMCs in India (as of 2024): 44 AMCs
AMFI Member List: https://www.amfiindia.com/amfi-members
SEBI Registration: All AMCs must be registered with SEBI under SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996

Regulatory Framework:
- Regulated by: SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)
- Industry Body: AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India)
- Trustee Oversight: Each AMC has an independent Trustee Company/Board of Trustees
